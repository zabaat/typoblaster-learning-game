# Science of Reading Word Notes

The word bank is designed for spelling practice that fits a structured literacy approach: students see a word, attend to the letter sequence, type each grapheme in order, and connect spelling to pronunciation and meaning through repeated accurate practice.

## Why These Words Fit

- **K-2:** short vowels, CVC words, consonant digraphs, blends, silent-e words, common vowel teams, r-controlled vowels, and simple two-syllable words.
- **3-5:** multisyllabic words, common prefixes and suffixes, vowel teams, r-controlled patterns, academic vocabulary, and morphology-friendly words.
- **6-8:** academic vocabulary, Greek/Latin-derived word parts, science vocabulary, morphology, multisyllabic decoding, and spelling patterns that reward chunking into syllables and morphemes.

## Pattern Examples

- **CVC / short vowels:** `cat`, `dog`, `sun`, `map`, `fin`
- **Digraphs:** `ship`, `fish`, `thin`, `whale`, `chair`
- **Blends:** `plant`, `grass`, `truck`, `slide`, `stream`
- **Silent e / long vowels:** `smile`, `brave`, `stone`, `shine`
- **Vowel teams:** `green`, `dream`, `train`, `paint`, `moon`
- **R-controlled vowels:** `star`, `corn`, `corner`, `harvest`
- **Two-syllable spelling:** `basket`, `rabbit`, `teacher`, `window`
- **Prefixes / suffixes:** `incorrect`, `carefully`, `movement`, `treatment`
- **Morphology / word parts:** `biology`, `geology`, `thermal`, `classification`
- **Academic science vocabulary:** `oxygen`, `gravity`, `fossil`, `ecosystem`, `photosynthesis`

## Sources Used For Alignment

- Reading Rockets explains phonics and decoding as letter-sound relationships used to read and spell words, and notes that spelling patterns support decoding.
- Reading Rockets' orthographic mapping guidance emphasizes connecting a word's letters, pronunciation, and meaning in memory rather than memorizing whole-word lists alone.
- The Institute of Education Sciences / What Works Clearinghouse foundational skills guide emphasizes phonological awareness, phonics, decoding, word-part analysis, and writing/recognizing words.

These sources support choosing words that highlight sound-spelling patterns, syllables, and meaningful word parts rather than arbitrary spelling lists.
